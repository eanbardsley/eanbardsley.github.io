4.0 Equilibrium States
The Earth receives radiation (light) from the Sun, some of that hits its surface and warms it,
another portion is reﬂected oﬀ the surface to the atmosphere where some of it is radiated out
into space and some is radiated back to the Earth’s surface warming it more. This amount held
in by greenhouse gases, like CO2 put into the atmosphere by industry and transportation
vehicles if is too much, beyond 350 parts per million of the air that makes up the atmosphere,
can put the Earth out of equilibrium and in a state where it is gaining more heat than it loses
raising the annual average temperature of the Earth. This is known as global warming and can
dry-up rivers and devastate crops. That is, if the Earth gains more radiation than it loses it is
warming, and if it loses more radiation than it is gaining it is cooling. If it loses as much
radiation as it gains it is said to be in equilibrium where the temperature remains constant. We
want the equilibrium state to be settled upon a temperature where rivers and crops don’t dry-
up.!
Similarly, we want to strive for being in an equilibrium state where our body weight is
concerned, where we burn on a daily basis as much food as we take in. However, we want that
the equilibrium state, that constant body weight, to be constant at a weight that is healthy.
However, if we want to settle at an equilibrium for a lower body weight, we have to go out of
equilibrium where we are losing more weight than we gain, daily, until we reach a weight we are
happy with. Once there, return to an equilibrium state where you are burning as much as you
gain. Which means now eating less than you did before, with perhaps a changed diet plan (A
