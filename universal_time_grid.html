Abstract: This paper explores the possibility that ancient civilizations possessed advanced
knowledge of Earth's dimensions and rotational dynamics, potentially contributing to a uniﬁed
planetary time grid. We analyze the alignment of Egyptian pyramids, particularly the spacing
between the Khufu and Khafre pyramids, in relation to Earth's rotation. This study also examines
the ancient Sumerians' base-60 mathematical system and its connection to time measurement.
Additionally, we investigate Eratosthenes' calculation of Earth's circumference, the role of the
Library of Alexandria in preserving this knowledge, and potential megalithic connections to
standardized units of measurement such as the 2.5 Megalithic Yard at Stonehenge. The ﬁndings
suggest a deeper, possibly interconnected understanding of planetary mechanics in ancient
civilizations.

circumference, derived from measurements in Egypt, closely matches both the estimated
separation of the pyramids and modern values of Earth's radius.

1. Introduction The division of time and space into standardized units has been an essential
aspect of human civilization. The ancient Sumerians, Egyptians, and Greeks each contributed to
the development of mathematical and astronomical systems that persist in modern timekeeping.
This paper explores whether these civilizations had a coherent understanding of planetary
rotation and spatial divisions based on Earth's circumference.
2. The Sumerian Base-60 System and the Second The Sumerians are credited with developing

second minutes. While there is no direct evidence that they had the technology to measure a
second, their mathematical framework laid the foundation for future civilizations. The
Babylonians later divided the circle into 360 degrees, further reinforcing a connection between
